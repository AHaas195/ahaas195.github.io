Introduction

In 1885, three tribal cheifs traveled to Great Britain in order to prevent Rhosesia (Zimbabwe) and the Union of South Africa from incorporating their land.

Great Britain put "Bechuanaland" under its protection, and then changed its name to Botswana.

Botswana has one of the most stable economies in Africa due to uninterrupted civilian leadership, the progression of social policies, and large capital investment.

Since the country gained independence, the Botswana Democratic Party has won every national election. The party continues to rule today.

President Mokgweesi Eric MASISI has been in power since 2018. He is the nation's fifth president.

Mineral extraction, espeically diamonds, are Botswana's main eceonomic activities. However, tourism is beginning to become more popular due to conservation practices.

Botswana has one of the world's highest HIV/AIDS infection rates. The country has developed one of Africa's most progressive programs for dealing with the disease.

Geography

Location

Botswana is located in Southern Africa, north of South Africa.

Terrain

The terrain is predominantly flat to general rolling tableland.

The Kalahari Desert is located in the southwest.

Natural Resources

Natural resources include:

  • Diamonds
  • Copper
  • Nickel
  • Salt
  • Soda ash
  • Potash
  • Coal
  • Iron ore
  • Silver

Environment

Climate

The climate in Botswana is semiarid, with warm winters and hot summers.

Current Issues

  • Overgrazing
  • Desertification
  • Limited freshwater resources
  • Air pollution

Demographic Profile

Botswana has experienced an extremely quick decline in fertilty in comparison to the rest of Sub-Sarahan Africa. The fertilty rate has fallen from 5 children per woman, to around 2.4 children. Reasons include...

  • Higer educatoin among women
  • More women are participating in the work force
  • Increased contraceptive use
  • Later births
  • Family planning programs

The prevalance rate of HIV/AIDS in Botswana is 22%, the third highest in the world.

Citizens of Botswana are called Batswana. Many Batswana prefer to live in their home country and will only cross borders for work on a temporary basis.
